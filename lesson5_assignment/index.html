Communication Through The Digital World

Overview | Media | Author

Overview

Ways to communicate have expanded throughout the years immensly with the help of technology. Now, most people have access to communicate easily with just a click of a button.Everyday people around the world use technology to communicate to others whether it be through texting, emailing, or even social media. Communication between humans developed along with the developments in science and technology which has lead to a universal widespread. In the 1800s, the invention of the telephone paved way for people to talk to eachother through technology. "Come here, I want to see you." was the first successful words spoken on the telephone. Now, people use their cellphones to not just call, but to text, tweet, and chat.

The usage of social networking has also skyrocketed throughout the generation. Sites such as Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter has been the go-to for communicating with friends and family on the internet. Social media users connect with others by following other people that use the platform, liking and sharing their updates, and commenting on their content. As followers increase, the amount of friends one has may increase as well. Thus, the way people communicate is amplyfied with the help of social media.

Not only is social media a giant communication platform; 75% of people in major countries have said they use texting as a form of communication. Not only is it helpful for just everyday communication, but fo remergencies as well. If there are any last minute canvellations, a physical emergency, or you just need help on anything, it is easy to contact another person through text.

Since communication technology is not that old, it is most popular in youth. Young people are more likely to join social media platforms and use their cellphones to ccommunicate with others that older people since this is the generation of technology. For example, 18 to 29 year olds are more likely to text and tweet more often than 50+ year olds. Thus, the population of communication technology is skewed towards the young.
